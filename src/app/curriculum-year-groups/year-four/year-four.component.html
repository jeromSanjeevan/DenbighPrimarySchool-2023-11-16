Year 4 Overview

Life in Year 4

In Year 4, students will be taught by Mrs. Diggines, Mrs. Semeraro, and Ms. Keshopersad. The curriculum for the year promises a rich and engaging learning experience across different subjects.

IPC

The first term delves into the fascinating world of Ancient Egypt, exploring historical connections with Ancient Sumer. The curriculum covers both human and physical geography, and art lessons involving the children creating Egyptian artifacts using a range of materials like pencil, clay, and paint.

Moving into the second half of the Autumn term, the focus shifts toward the scientific exploration of electricity. Students gain hands-on experience by designing and constructing model robots incorporating circuits, with lights, sounds, and movement without the topic of "Bright Sparks”. The rest of the term continues with "How Humans Work" with an exploration of scientific topics such, as nutrition, digestion, and skeletons.

As the academic year progresses, in the Spring term the curriculum shifts towards "The Romans" in the third term. This section explores the impact of the Roman Empire on Britain, covering historical aspects and integrating geography to understand both physical and human elements. In the second half of the term, we begin “Go with the Flow," where students dive into scientific concepts of the water cycle, paralleled with a geographic exploration of physical and human aspects. Design and Technology (DT) takes on a hands-on approach, incorporating woodwork and mechanisms into the curriculum.

In the Summer term, "Look Hear," focuses on the scientific principles of sound, particularly understanding pitch and volume. This three-week module provides a concentrated exploration of auditory concepts. The final term explores the theme of "Vanishing Rainforests." Integrating geography, science, and history, students delve into concepts such as hemispheres, the equator, climate zones, and biomes. The curriculum also touches on the

achievements of non-European civilizations, drawing connections with British history, as seen through the growth and importance of the cocoa trade. Design and Technology (DT) take a culinary turn, with students engaging in cooking and nutrition activities, designing tropical fruit drinks, and even creating chocolates.

The multiplication tables check (MTC) is statutory for all year 4 pupils The purpose of the MTC is to determine whether pupils can recall their times tables fluently, which is essential for future success in mathematics. It will help schools to identify pupils who have not yet mastered their times tables so that additional support can be provided

In 2024, schools must administer the MTC to all eligible year 4 pupils between Monday 3 June and Friday 14 June

Starting the school year, we will begin reading "Pharaoh in the Bath" by Jeremy Strong. We'll be writing about the main character, Pharaoh, by describing him, creating fact files, writing diary entries, news reports, and instructions. Pharaoh's quirky adventures will be our inspiration. We'll explore his appearance and personality, and dive into the imaginative world crafted by Jeremy Strong. Our writing will let us experience Pharaoh's thoughts and adventures, from capturing the craziness in news reports to creating step-by-step instructions for mummification

In the second half of the autumn term, we will be reading "The Wild Robot" by Peter Brown. Our writing focus will include describing the setting, crafting non-chronological reports, storytelling, and engaging in a debate about artificial intelligence.

In the Spring term, we are immersing ourselves in the magical world of "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone." As we explore the enchanting narrative, our writing endeavours will encompass a variety of styles. We will master the art of formal letter writing, capturing the essence of our favourite characters and their experiences at Hogwarts. Additionally, we will channel our creativity into crafting poetry inspired by the wizarding wonders that unfold within the pages. Furthermore, we'll dive into the meticulous world of instruction writing, providing clear and concise guidelines as if we were wizards in training. The pinnacle of our journey will be a visit to the Harry Potter Studios, where we'll draw inspiration for our writing from the captivating sets and magical atmosphere. Get ready for a term filled with literary magic!

Writing in our final term we will base our writing on "Journey" by Aaron Becker. We'll be having fun with playwriting, turning the exciting story into a script. Students will get to act out the characters and bring the adventure to life on paper. Alongside this, we're also writing adventure stories, exploring our imaginative tales of discovery.
