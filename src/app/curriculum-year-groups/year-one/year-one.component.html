Year 1 Overview

Welcome to Year 1 at Denbigh Primary School!

This year, your child will move onto the learning based around the National Curriculum and also take part in continuous provision. Continuous provision is all of the different areas which are available for your children to use every day. Within each of these areas of provision there are a core range of resources that children can use all of the time, throughout the whole year which will challenge their thinking and help them to embed concepts.

English

In Year 1, we build on the childrenâ€™s learning from the Read, Write Inc. phonics programme in EYFS and teach the children to read further sounds, words and storybooks to develop their word reading and comprehension skills.

They develop speedy reading and apply their sound knowledge by reading nonsense words. In June, pupils in Year 1 take the government Phonics Screening Check (https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/phonics-screening-check-information-for-parents) where they will read 20 real words and 20 nonsense words.

The Read, Write Inc. programme is excellent in teaching our children the sounds and skills they need for this. In previous years, the pass mark has been 32/40 (but this is not released until after pupils have taken the check and could change).

Children Will:
  • Consolidate their learning of phonics
  • Be encouraged to read a wide range of stories and listen to poems which they will start to recite by heart
  • Learn to spell a range of words containing the sounds already taught (e.g., light, read, think, etc.)
  • Learn to form all the letters of the alphabet in lower case and capitals, plus the digits 0 to 9
  • Sequence sentences to form short stories
  • Punctuate sentences with a capital and full stop and will begin to learn about question marks and exclamation marks

Mathematics

Children Will:
  • Count forwards and backwards up to 100
  • Know their addition and subtraction facts to 20
  • Start to learn about times tables through the use of simple multiplication and division problems, for which they will be given objects to help them work out what is being asked
  • Find half and a quarter of a shape or quantity
  • Start to measure using standard units and will learn to tell the time to the hour and half-hour
  • Learn to name some common 2D and 3D shapes
